# KickSnap Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 12 July 2026**

This Privacy Policy explains how **KickSnap** ("KickSnap", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects your personal data when you use the KickSnap website and application at **kicksnap.net** (the "Service").

KickSnap is operated from the **United Kingdom**, but our users are global. We act as the **data controller** for the personal data described below. We comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and, where they apply to you, the EU GDPR and other applicable data protection laws.

If you have any questions, contact us at **privacy@kicksnap.net**.

---

## 1. The most important thing: we never receive your videos

KickSnap edits your clips **entirely inside your own web browser** using the WebCodecs API. Your video files are **never uploaded to, transmitted to, or stored on our servers or any third party's servers**. Importing, trimming, adding overlays, and exporting all happen locally on your device. When you close the tab, the clip is gone from our reach because it was never in it.

As a result, **we do not collect, process, or have any access to the contents of your video clips.** This Policy covers only the account, payment, and usage data described below.

---

## 2. Data we collect

### 2.1 Account and authentication data (via Discord)
You sign in using **Discord OAuth**. When you do, Discord shares a limited set of data with us based on the permissions you approve, which may include:
- your Discord user ID;
- your Discord username and display/global name;
- your Discord avatar image; and
- the email address associated with your Discord account (if you grant the email permission).

We do **not** receive your Discord password. Your use of Discord is governed by [Discord's own privacy policy](https://discord.com/privacy).

### 2.2 Credit, subscription, and transaction data
To run the credit system we store:
- your current credit balance and plan/tier;
- records of credit purchases, subscriptions, and credits consumed on export; and
- limited transaction metadata received from our payment processor (see below).

### 2.3 Payment data (via Stripe)
Payments are processed by **Stripe** ("Stripe"). You enter your card details directly with Stripe — **we never see or store your full card number.** Stripe shares limited data with us such as a transaction/customer ID, subscription status, the card's last four digits, card brand, and billing country. Stripe processes your data under [Stripe's privacy policy](https://stripe.com/privacy).

### 2.4 Usage and analytics data
We use **PostHog** to understand how the Service is used and to improve it. This may include pages/screens visited, features used, buttons clicked, approximate location (derived from IP), device and browser type, referring URL, and timestamps. This **product analytics does not include the content of your clips.** See our [Cookie Policy](./cookie-policy.md) for details on the cookies and local storage involved.

### 2.5 Technical and log data
Our hosting and infrastructure providers automatically process technical data such as your IP address, request logs, and security/error logs to deliver and protect the Service.

### 2.6 Communications
If you contact us (e.g. by email or in our Discord community), we process the information you choose to share so we can respond and keep records of support requests.

### 2.7 Local storage on your device
The Service stores small pieces of data in your browser (e.g. sign-in session tokens and preference flags such as whether you have claimed the Discord reward). See the [Cookie Policy](./cookie-policy.md).

We do **not** knowingly collect special-category data (e.g. health, biometric, or political data), and you should not enter such data into the Service.

## 4. Who we share data with

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with:

- **Service providers (processors)** who act on our instructions, including:
  - **Vercel** — website and application hosting / content delivery;
  - **Supabase** — authentication and database hosting (stores your account and credit data);
  - **Stripe** — payment processing;
  - **PostHog** — product analytics;
  - **Discord** — sign-in (OAuth) and community.
- **Authorities and advisers** where required by law, to enforce our Terms, or to protect our rights, safety, or property.
- **A successor** in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

A current list of sub-processors is available on request at **privacy@kicksnap.net**.

## 6. How long we keep data

We keep personal data only as long as necessary:
- **Account data:** for as long as your account is active, and for a reasonable period afterwards.
- **Transaction and tax records:** typically **six (6) years** to meet UK legal, tax, and accounting requirements.
- **Analytics data:** for a limited retention period configured in PostHog.
- **Support communications:** as long as needed to handle the matter and keep reasonable records.

Because we never receive your clips, there is nothing to retain or delete regarding your video content.

## 7. How we protect your data

We use technical and organisational measures appropriate to the risk, including encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS), access controls, reputable infrastructure providers, and minimising the data we hold. No system is perfectly secure, but our client-side processing model means the most sensitive data — your clips — never leaves your device.

## 8. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you have some or all of the following rights.

### 8.1 UK / EU (UK GDPR and EU GDPR)
- **Access** – get a copy of your data.
- **Rectification** – correct inaccurate data.
- **Erasure** – ask us to delete your data ("right to be forgotten").
- **Restriction** – limit how we use your data.
- **Portability** – receive your data in a portable format.
- **Objection** – object to processing based on legitimate interests or to direct marketing.
- **Withdraw consent** – at any time, where we rely on consent.
- **Complain** – to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at [ico.org.uk](https://ico.org.uk), or your local supervisory authority.

### 8.2 California (CCPA/CPRA)
California residents may request to know, delete, or correct their personal information, and to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information. **We do not sell or share your personal information** in the sense defined by these laws. You will not be discriminated against for exercising your rights.

### 8.3 Everyone else
If your local law grants you data rights, we will honour them to the extent they apply. Contact us and we'll do our best to help.

To exercise any right, email **privacy@kicksnap.net**. We may need to verify your identity, and we will respond within the timeframes required by law (generally one month under UK GDPR).
